My datasets

Balance of Payments/International Investment Position (xlsx)

This workbook contains time series of the major items of the Balance of Payments and International Investment Position of 95 countries. The starting date varies by country. For the annual data, the earliest starting year is 1960, while for the quarterly data the earliest starting date is 1989:Q1. The first sheet, "boo-iip template," maps the conventional presentation of the BOP and the IIP onto variable codes, using Australia as an example. The second sheet, "list of all BOP-IIP variables," lists all the variables in the IMF's BOP/IIP database, with a description. The original source is the IMF's BOP database: http://data.imf.org/BOP. This version: 9/23/2019.


External datasets

Real commodity prices

Description: Real prices of 40 commodities, as well as three indices (value-of-production weights at 1975 and 2011 shares, and equal weights), and three sub-indices (commodities to be grown, commodities in the ground, and commodities in the ground, ex-energy). The indices and sub-indices start in 1900. Each individual price series is expressed in U.S. dollars and deflated by the U.S. CPI.

Period: 1850-2018.

Documentation or metadata: David Jacks' site and Jacks, D.S. (2019), "From Boom to Bust: A Typology of Real Commodity Prices in the Long Run," Cliometrica, 13(2), 202-220. (Working paper version at NBER).

Link to data: xlsx


Standardized world inequality database

Description: Gini indices for 196 countries.

Period: Varies by country. The earliest year is 1960. Version 8.1 extends the dataset to 2018 or latest available year.

Documentation or metadata: Frederick Solt's site.

Link to data: Dataset repository at Harvard Dataverse.


REER and NEER for 178 countries

Description: Real and nominal effective exchange rates, based on the CPI. The database also contains a quarterly REER index based on unit labor costs (ULC). There are three versions of the ULC-based index: one for the total economy, one for the business sector ex. agriculture, construction, and real estate, and one for manufacturing.

Period: Varies by country and frequency. The narrow index, at annual (monthly) frequency, starts in 1960 (1970) at the earliest, and the broad index starts in 1995 (1993).

Documentation or metadata: Darvas, Zsolt (2012a) ‘Real effective exchange rates for 178 countries: a new database‘, Working Paper 2012/06, Bruegel, 15 March 2012.

Link to data: Dataset repository at Bruegel.


Government revenue database

Description: General and central government revenue, for 196 countries. Different revenue aggregates and sub-components are presented: overall revenue; tax revenue; resource taxes; social contributions; taxes on individual and corporate income; VAT taxes; taxes on imports and exports; etc. For panel data analysis, the dataset recommends using "Total government revenue, excluding grants and social contributions," as this is the most consistent and complete measure across countries. All series are expressed as percentages of GDP, and NGDP series are included as well.

Period: Varies by country, with the earliest starting year being 1980.

Documentation or metadata: GRD Documentation

Link to data: xlsx.


Long-term productivity database

Description: Labor productivity, TFP, capital intensity, and average age of equipment for 23 countries. The site has now a tool to build productivity scenarios through 2100.

Period: 1870-2016 for most countries.

Documentation or metadata: Database site, and Bergeaud, A., Cette, G. and Lecat, R. (2016): "Productivity Trends in Advanced Countries between 1890 and 2012," Review of Income and Wealth, 62(3), 420–444.

Link to data: xlsx.


REER during the Gold Standard

Description: Real effective exchange rates of 16 countries.

Period: 1870-1913 for most countries.

Documentation or metadata: AER article site, and Catão, Luis, A. V., and Solomos N. Solomou (2005), "Effective Exchange Rates and the Classical Gold Standard Adjustment," American Economic Review, 95(4), 1259-1275. The construction of the indices is described only in the online appendix.

Link to data: zip.
